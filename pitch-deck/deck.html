For ground & cabin crews

Say it once.
They hear it in their language.

AeroTranslate is a real-time, two-way speech translator for airline ground and cabin crews — no shared language required, no app for the passenger to install.

The problem

Airports run on language — and constantly run out of it

01Ground and cabin crews deal with passengers who don't speak their language every shift, at every gate — boarding changes, baggage issues, missed connections, medical situations.
02Today's fallback is slow and incomplete: find a bilingual colleague, call a phone interpretation line, or hand over a phone and hope a translation app gets the meaning across.
03The cost shows up as longer interactions, escalations, missed flights, and staff who can't do their job with full confidence — in an industry where minutes at the gate matter.
04This isn't a rare edge case. It's a daily, recurring operational gap at every international airport.
AeroTranslate
02
The solution

A live translator that lives in the conversation, not an app passengers have to find

01Staff speaks naturally into a tablet — no scripts, no menus, no waiting for an interpreter to be free.
02Passenger scans a QR code with their own phone — no app install, no account, no setup.
03Speech is translated in real time and delivered both as text and spoken aloud, in both directions.
04Built on real-time, speech-to-speech AI (Gemini live audio) — not a text-translation app retrofitted for conversation.
AeroTranslate

How it works

Three steps. Zero shared language.

01
Staff speaks naturally
Tap once, talk like normal — no scripts, no menus, no waiting for a translator to be free.
02
Translated instantly — shown and spoken
The passenger sees the translation on their phone and hears it spoken aloud in their own language, seconds later.
03
Passenger replies the same way
They tap their mic and answer back — translated into staff's language, read aloud right there on the tablet.

Why now

This works now. It didn't five years ago.

01Real-time speech-to-speech AI finally clears the bar. Latency and accuracy are now low and high enough for live, face-to-face conversation — not just batch text translation.
02Passengers already carry the only hardware needed. QR-pairing to a personal phone means zero install, zero friction, and zero devices for an airline to buy, issue, or maintain.
03Staffing pressure makes "hire more bilingual staff" an expensive, slow lever. Software is the lever that scales across every counter and every shift.

Market & business model

Where this fits, and how it makes money

Market
Global air travel moves billions of passengers a year through thousands of airports, and the language services industry built to bridge gaps like this is itself estimated in the tens of billions of dollars annually (industry estimates, e.g. CSA Research / Nimdzi) — without a tool built for live, two-way, no-install conversation at the gate.

Buyers: airlines, ground handling companies, airports, and lounges — anywhere staff and passengers need to communicate without a shared language.
Business model (planned)
B2B subscription priced per active counter or per agent seat, tiered by usage volume and language coverage.

Enterprise tier: custom vocabulary for boarding announcements and aviation terminology, plus usage reporting for ops teams.
